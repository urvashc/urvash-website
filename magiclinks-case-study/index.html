Prioritization
Why This Order, Specifically At MagicLinks
1
Fix Reporting Data
Campaign Success touches every renewal conversation. Inaccurate dashboards at renewal time aren't a support ticket, they're a churn event. 2
Fix Mobile (Parallel Track)
The brief says the app is "not working well" for link creation and transaction tracking looks off. Top creators can't earn reliably right now. This runs in parallel with data: different engineers, different problem space. I won't conflate "not working well" with a specific root cause until I've investigated. The fix path determines the timeline.
3
Build Brand Portal
Sales is losing deals on a specific, named gap. But I won't scope this from assumptions. Before writing a single feature, I'll listen to multiple lost-deal sales calls, run deep-dives with the sales team, reach out to lost customers and at-risk renewals directly, and do a full competitor teardown (Aspire, LTK, impact.com). Only after that does a v2 hypothesis become defensible. Clean data from phase 1 is a prerequisite: 30 days of stable reporting unlocks the design sprint.

Phase 1, Track 1
Data Accuracy: How I'll Actually Diagnose It
1
Week 1: Observe Before Hypothesizing. I join Campaign Success for live renewal preps. Not to read the Jira tickets: to watch where they open a second tab. That pause is the actual bug report. I map every manual step and note exactly which fields they cross-check. By Friday I have a hypothesis: attribution window? Deduplication? Timezone handling? I state it explicitly, then test it with data.
2
Week 2: Find The One Place. I pull raw event logs with Tech Lead + Eng 2 at every pipeline stage: ingestion, transformation, aggregation. We build a comparison table for 5 real campaigns, source event count vs. dashboard output. The discrepancy points to one specific place: a join, a cache, a window mismatch. That one place. Not a broad rewrite.
3
Week 3: Define, Fix, Communicate. In That Order. I write the definition of "correct metric" jointly with Campaign Success before a line of code is touched. This is a joint decision, not an engineering spec. Fix deployed. Client communication drafted: what changed, what it means for historical data, what they should expect. Campaign Success co-signs the outreach, not a handoff.
4
Week 4: Verify Behavior, Not The Dashboard. The real signal isn't "numbers look right." It's: did Campaign Success stop the manual cross-check? I count steps per renewal prep. Target: under 5. Still reconciling means my diagnosis was incomplete. I go back to Week 2.

Phase 1, Track 2
Mobile P0: Diagnose Before Committing To A Timeline
The brief says the app is "not working well" for link creation and "transaction tracking looks off." I won't label this a crash or a data bug until I know which it is. A rendering issue takes days. An API contract break takes weeks. Transaction "looking off" could be display rounding (hours) or a pipeline issue (weeks). These are completely different fix paths. I won't commit to a date before I know what I'm fixing.
Weekly Investigation Plan
Week 1
Reproduce + Characterize
Eng 3 pulls error logs, segments by device OS, app version, and creator tier. I reach out directly to the highest-revenue creators affected: exact sequence, exact screen, what happens next. Reproduce in dev environment by day 5.

Hypothesis by day 5: client-side rendering issue or server-side API/data issue? Designer maps both broken interaction flows before any code is written.
Week 2
Root Cause + Fix Path Locked
Tech Lead + Eng 3 confirm root cause. Two paths:
(A) Rendering bug: fix ships in week 3. Designer validates UX before release.
(B) API/data issue: 3-week coordinated fix with different resource allocation.

Transaction tracking traced separately. Display rounding or upstream data? Public timeline only after root cause is confirmed.
Week 3+
Fix, Validate, Confirm
Fix validated with the highest-revenue creators affected first, before any wide release. Three sign-offs required before wide release:
(1) Creator confirms it works.
(2) Campaign Success confirms transaction view matches admin panel.
(3) Designer validates no adjacent flow breaks.

Not done until all three confirm.
Design Approach
Designer's Role (Not Just Review)
Designer is involved from day 1, not called in after engineering is done. They map the broken interaction before Eng 3 writes code, specify the corrected flow, and own UX validation of the fix. Even a bug fix can break adjacent flows; a designer spec prevents that.
AI-Accelerated Prototyping
Designer uses Claude or v0.dev to generate functional UI prototypes of the corrected flow in hours, not days. Share async with the top affected creators via Maze or Loom. Collect structured feedback in 48hrs. Iterate before production code is written. Reduces rework and proves the fix works before it ships.

When Does The Portal Pause?
Simple rule: if something is breaking current revenue or visible to existing clients within 7 days, the portal pauses.

I write a trade-off memo to leadership within 24hrs: what drops, what it costs in pipeline, and what signal brings it back. The memo goes out before any scope changes, not after.

The portal is in month 2 specifically because it can pause. Data and mobile cannot. This is not an accident. It's the sequencing decision.
Phase 3: Days 61-90
Match Intelligence v2: Understand v1 Before Designing v2
The CEO has asked for v2. Before I design anything, I run a structured discovery. A v2 built on assumptions about v1 is a v2 built on sand. The 30-day data stability window in phase 1 exists precisely to make this discovery possible and the answers trustworthy.
1
What Is v1 Actually Doing Today?
What data inputs does it use: follower count, engagement rate, category tags, historical campaign performance? I'll work with the campaign performance team to understand what they observe in campaign outcomes vs. what v1 predicted. Where is v1 getting it wrong?
2
What's The Baseline ROI And The CEO's Target?
What is the current match-to-conversion rate? What lift does the CEO specifically expect from v2? Are we optimizing for brand campaign ROI, creator earnings, or platform GMV? These are different objective functions with different models.
3
What Data Is v1 Missing?
Hypothesis: v1 uses audience-side signals. v2 should use purchase-intent signals: what a creator's audience actually buys, not how many follow them. MagicLinks has years of transaction data at scale. No competitor has this. Does our data layer expose it cleanly enough to use?
4
What Do Brands And Creators Actually Want?
The brand and creator teams are closest to this feedback. Top creator conversations reveal what better matching means to them. Are we solving for their problem, or the platform's optimization metric? These aren't always the same answer.
Working Hypothesis (Post-Discovery)
v1 ranks on audience-side signals. v2 bets that purchase-intent signal: what a creator's audience actually buys is a stronger ROI predictor. MagicLinks has transaction data at scale that no competitor can replicate. If the hypothesis holds, Match Intelligence v2 isn't just a feature upgrade. It's the beginning of a defensible moat: a compounding, proprietary asset built from every campaign that runs on the platform.

Transparency
What I'm Explicitly Not Doing In 90 Days: And Why
Match Intelligence v2 Build
Discovery work in phase 2 and design sprint in phase 3 are prerequisites. Building v2 before completing discovery produces a worse algorithm faster. The day 90 go/no-go recommendation is what unlocks the build, not impatience.
Brand Portal v2 Features
Custom reporting, white-labeling, API access, multi-user permissions. If none of these appeared in lost-deal debriefs as explicit blockers, they don't ship in MVP. Revisit after beta brands tell us what they actually need next, not the backlog.
Mobile App Redesign
Phase 1 scope is link creation and transaction tracking, what the brief flags. A full redesign is a multi-quarter initiative requiring a separate resourcing conversation. Conflating redesign with P0 fixes loses both.
Cross-Product Analytics Platform
Requires dedicated analytics engineering capacity this squad doesn't have. Right diagnosis, wrong time, wrong resourcing. This is a roadmap conversation for Q3 at the earliest.
Trade-Off Log Protocol
Every dropped item gets three things in writing: what it costs (in pipeline, retention, or velocity), the explicit condition that brings it back (e.g., "if Match Intelligence v2 go/no-go is approved, build starts in Q3"), and a timestamp. This log is public, searchable, and referenced in every roadmap review. No verbal updates, no Slack pings treated as decisions. Written, accountable, auditable.
Communication
AI-Augmented, Bidirectional, Async-First
The brief calls out company-wide frustration with product visibility. The solution isn't more meetings. It's a communication system that's always on and works in both directions.
WeeklyAI-DraftedEvery Friday · Public Slack Channel
Claude drafts the decision log from my notes each week. I review and ship it. Not a status update: a decision artifact. What I know, what I don't, what changed, what dropped and why. Searchable and linkable: future disagreements get resolved by pointing at the log, not by relitigating. Anyone in the company can see what product is doing without asking.
MonthlyAI-SummarizedOne-Page Roadmap Letter · Leadership
AI summarizes what shipped, what dropped, what changed since last month. I write the "why this bet" section by hand. That part can't be delegated. Leadership reads it in 3 minutes and knows exactly what product is optimizing for and what trade-offs were made.
QuarterlyTrade-Off Presentation · All Stakeholders
3 bets I'm fully committed to. Everything else on the deferred list with explicit revival conditions. Stakeholders see what I considered and rejected, not just what I chose. This is what builds trust over time, not just delivery, but the reasoning behind what didn't ship.
Stakeholder Feedback Loop
I send structured prompts, not broadcasts. "Sales: does this portal scope match what you heard in last week's calls?" Specific owner, specific question, tracked response. Silence is a signal: if a stakeholder stops engaging with my updates, I flag it as a relationship risk before it becomes an escalation.
Handling Pushback
When pushback arrives, "why isn't Match Intelligence v2 shipping?" or "the portal scope is wrong," I respond in writing within 24hrs: acknowledge the concern, explain the trade-off, confirm the next step. Every response is logged. No disagreement goes unresolved in Slack threads. Written, dated, owned.
